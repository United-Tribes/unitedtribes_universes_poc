**Research:** In the Apple TV+ show Pluribus (2025), the 15th-century choral piece "Deo gratias" by Johannes Ockeghem (performed by the Huelgas Ensemble conducted by Paul Van Nevel) is used to score a pivotal, silent, and atmospheric scene in Episode 5, "Got Milk". Scene Context: The music plays during a scene described as one of the most beautiful in the series, featuring a silent, slow-motion exodus of "the Others" leaving Albuquerque. As Carol plots her next move and wins $10,000 on a scratch-off game (which means nothing in Pluribus world!) Baltmora's "Tarzan Boy" plays through the speaker and helps set the mood. Lyrics about monkey business, other worlds, a signal to the sky, playing alone, and joining and surviving? Extremely Pluribus coded! Plus, an invigorating beat!

**Short:** Ep 7 'The Gap' — Carol lights fireworks in her cul-de-sac humming Sousa's march, then later sits in the center of an explosive ring as the Navy Band performance blasts. One tilts toward her — she stares it down, unafraid, seemingly welcoming death.

Later in "The Gap," a U.S. Navy Band performance of the song blasts as she sits in the middle of another ring of fireworks. As they start to go off, one tilts directly towards her. Rather than racing to reposition it, Carol stares straight on, unafraid and seemingly welcoming death. The explosive narrowly misses her, and she snaps out of her morbid trance to put out the blaze, but the grandeur of song undoubtedly raised the stakes of the scene and added a welcome comedic flair.
