# Margaret Qualley's Closet Picks

**Title:** Margaret Qualley's Closet Picks
**Source:** CRITERION
**ID:** Ddh6FBvHFi0
**Duration:** 02:25
**Type:** Interview/Closet Picks

Margaret Qualley spends two minutes and twenty-five seconds in the Criterion Closet pulling six movies that map the territory between her dance training and her acting career. She opens with Claire Denis's Beau travail and singles out Denis Lavant's closing dance sequence — Qualley says she is "obsessed" with him — without mentioning that she herself starred in a Claire Denis film called Stars at Noon. She moves through Gus Van Sant's To Die For, quoting Nicole Kidman's "bringing the world into our homes" monologue from memory and calling Kidman's performance "perfectly psychotic" [01:05]. She quotes Adam Sandler's Mattress Man speech from Punch-Drunk Love verbatim and uses the moment to argue that Happy Gilmore and Big Daddy belong on Criterion shelves [01:21]. She declares Bob Fosse's All That Jazz the greatest movie ever made and says she would want to dance with Fosse rather than have dinner with him [02:09]. She closes with Ridley Scott's Thelma & Louise and states outright that she wants to make a female road-trip movie of her own [02:21]. Five seconds on All About Eve in the middle. Throughout, dance is the throughline — Qualley's two strongest endorsements both center on movement, and the entire visit reads as a working-actor's notebook of physical performance.
