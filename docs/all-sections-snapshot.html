Rhea Seehorn

REP
as Carol Sturka
Born: May 12, 1972 Norfolk, Virginia, USA Acting
In Pluribus — Carol Sturka

Carol Sturka is a recently widowed woman who begins experiencing a phenomenon she cannot explain: she can hear the thoughts of those around her. As she navigates grief, she becomes the reluctant center of a global conspiracy involving hive mind technology, government cover-ups, and the question of whether individual consciousness can survive collective absorption. Seehorn brings the same moral precision she brought to Kim Wexler — Carol is watchful, intelligent, and quietly devastated.

About Rhea Seehorn

Rhea Seehorn is an American actress born on May 12, 1972, in Norfolk, Virginia. She is best known for her critically acclaimed portrayal of Kim Wexler in the AMC drama series Better Call Saul, a role which earned her widespread recognition and multiple award nominations — including the Golden Globe she accepted in 2022.

Before her breakthrough, Seehorn worked steadily in television, appearing in shows including Whitney, Franklin & Bash, and Burn Notice. She studied at the Governor's School for the Arts and later trained extensively in Washington D.C. and Los Angeles, developing a disciplined, internally-focused technique that critics have noted for its extraordinary specificity and restraint.

— SECTION 3c: CREW DETAIL (Vince Gilligan) —

Vince Gilligan

CREATOR

Vince Gilligan is an American writer, director, and producer. He created the critically-acclaimed Breaking Bad and its spin-off Better Call Saul, and previously served as writer and producer on The X-Files for seven seasons.

About Vince Gilligan

Vincent Gilligan was born on February 10, 1967, in Farmville, Virginia. He studied film at New York University's Tisch School of the Arts, graduating in 1989. He broke into Hollywood writing for The X-Files in 1995, where he worked for seven seasons and became a key figure in the writers' room — creating fan-favorite episodes including "Bad Blood," "Pusher," and "Drive," which famously featured a pre-fame Bryan Cranston.

His landmark achievement remains Breaking Bad, which premiered on AMC in 2008. The show ran for five seasons and is widely considered one of the greatest television dramas ever made, winning 16 Emmy Awards. Its prequel, Better Call Saul, ran from 2015 to 2022 and is considered by many critics to surpass even its predecessor. Pluribus, his first project since BCS, reunites him with collaborators spanning his entire career.
